Model Choice Rationale
Why linear regression, not a neural network or per-person AI agents
Why not a neural network?
A neural network requires thousands of training samples to learn non-linear relationships without overfitting. We have 33 ZIP codes. At that scale, a neural network would memorize the training data and produce predictions with no statistical validity, a textbook overfitting problem. Linear regression with 5 predictors on 33 samples yields an R² of 0.74, which is statistically sound and fully defensible. The neural network architecture becomes appropriate at the census-tract level where thousands of data points across multiple cities provide the density required.
Why not an AI agent per person?
Jacksonville's desert ZIPs contain over 250,000 residents. A personalized AI agent per person using a frontier model API costs roughly $0.01 to $0.05 per interaction. Daily check-ins alone would cost $912K to $4.5M per year in API fees, before staff or infrastructure. That same budget funds 8 to 40 actual nurse practitioners. Our solutions use AI at the triage and routing layer, one model serving thousands, which is where AI delivers maximum value at minimum cost.
